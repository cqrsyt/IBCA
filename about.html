About IBCA

Identity and foundational vision of the International Bank of Climate Adaptation

Identity

Full Name: International Bank of Climate Adaptation (IBCA)

Headquarters: Geneva, Switzerland

IBCA is a proposed treaty-based international financial institution established to address the critical gap in climate adaptation finance for the world’s most vulnerable countries.

Why IBCA Exists

Adverse impacts of climate change — particularly extreme weather events, sea-level rise, and ecosystem degradation — disproportionately threaten Least Developed Countries (LDCs) and Small Island Developing States (SIDS).

Existing international financial institutions and climate funds have inadequately addressed the urgent adaptation needs of the most vulnerable. IBCA is designed as a dedicated institution focused exclusively on climate adaptation, founded on sound banking principles and strictly precluded from diversifying its mandate.

Core Design Principles

Narrow Mandate

Legally binding exclusive purpose prevents mission creep. Any amendment requires the highest voting threshold.

Sound Banking

Operates on sound banking principles while prioritising the most climate-vulnerable nations.

Win-Win Cooperation

Empowers powerful states to support vulnerable states through innovative governance while maintaining robust financial standing.

Complementarity

Designed to cooperate with existing specialised organisations (including WMO and UN agencies) and non-State stakeholders to maximise synergies and avoid duplication.
